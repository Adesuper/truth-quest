The 9th Commandment

"You shall not give false testimony against your neighbor."

— Exodus 20:16

This means: Do not lie. God wants us to always tell the truth, even when it feels hard or scary.

🤔

Why Does Truth Matter?

  • 💙 Truth builds trust — When we're honest, people can believe in us
  • 🤝 Truth builds friendships — Real friends tell each other the truth
  • 😌 Truth gives us peace — No guilt, no hiding, no worry
  • ✝️ Truth follows Jesus — Jesus IS the truth (John 14:6)!
💔

What Happens When We Lie?

  • 😟 It breaks trust — People stop believing us
  • 😣 It hurts others — Lies can make people feel sad and confused
  • 😔 It hurts us too — Lying gives us a heavy, guilty feeling inside
  • 🔗 It grows bigger — One lie often leads to more lies

✝Adventurers

💬 Let's Talk About It!

Before we take the quiz, let's think about Sam's story together.

💡

Leader tip: Read each question aloud. Let a few children answer first, then tap "Show Suggested Answer" to wrap up each one.

1

Has anyone ever been in a situation like Sam's — where you broke something or made a mistake and had to decide whether to tell the truth?

It's normal to feel scared when we make a mistake. But that's exactly when choosing truth matters most — we might face consequences, but we keep something more valuable: trust.

2

How do you feel inside when you tell a lie? How about when you tell the truth — even when it's hard?

Lying gives us a heavy, guilty feeling — like a weight on our chest. Telling the truth feels scary at first, but then comes a warm peace inside — that's God saying "You did the right thing."

3

Why do you think God made "Do not lie" one of the Ten Commandments? Why is it so important to Him?

Because God is truth, and lying destroys the relationships He created us to enjoy. Truth is the foundation of love and trust — without it, friendships and families break apart.

4

Jesus said "I am the truth." What do you think that means for us when we follow Him?

It means everything about Jesus is real and trustworthy — He never lied and never broke a promise. When we follow Him, we choose to be truth-tellers just like He is.
